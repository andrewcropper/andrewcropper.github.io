cropper |ˈkrɒpə|

noun

  1. a plant which yields a specified crop: the white-fleshed varieties are the heaviest croppers.
  2. a machine or person that cuts or trims something.
  3. chiefly a person who raises a crop, especially as a sharecropper.
  4. CS PhD student at Imperial College London.

PHRASES

come a cropper Brit. informal fall heavily. he came the most appalling cropper—I think he knocked himself out. • suffer a defeat or disaster: the club’s challenge for the championship has come a cropper.
